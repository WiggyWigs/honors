Carlos Flores

Championships 0
Career Earnings -$725.50 (5th)
Career Grand Slam N/A — missing: The Open Championship

Carlos has been a fixture of The Honors since its founding in 2019, and his career numbers tell the story of a player who's dangerous but inconsistent — 37 event wins is respectable, but his career winning percentage and made-cut rate both sit at the bottom of the five-man field. What keeps him relevant is his major championship pedigree: six career major titles, including The Masters and The PGA Championship in the same 2022 season, put him one Open Championship away from completing the career Grand Slam. He's never finished atop the season standings, but he's rarely far from the majors conversation, and at this point the missing Open Championship is as much a running storyline as it is a stat.

Greg Wigton

Click photo for full stats
Championships 2 (2022, 2023)
Career Earnings $449.50 (2nd)
Career Grand Slam N/A — missing: The PGA Championship

As the league's founder and commissioner, Greg has spent as much time running The Honors as playing in it — and he's still managed to win it twice, taking back-to-back titles in 2022 and 2023, the only member to go back-to-back. His career quality lineup rate is the best in the league, and his made-cut rate trails only Kevin McNamara. Five career majors and 44 event wins round out a resume that's more complete than flashy: Greg tends to win by avoiding disaster weeks more consistently than anyone else in the field, even if he's never quite caught William or Kevin in the major-count column. The missing PGA Championship is the one gap standing between him and a career Grand Slam.

Kevin McNamara

Click photo for full stats
Championships 4 (2019, 2021, 2024, 2025)
Career Earnings $1,407.00 (1st)
Career Grand Slam 2023

Kevin is the standard the other four measure themselves against. His four championships are the most in league history, and he enters every season with the best career winning percentage and made-cut rate in the field. He was also the first member to complete the career Grand Slam, closing it out in 2023, and has since started chasing a second cycle, already picking up The Open Championship and U.S. Open again. The one blemish on the resume: his otherwise strong 2020 season also produced the league's all-time worst single-season last-place record, a reminder that even the league's most consistent player has had a genuinely bad stretch.

Robert Bresnahan

Championships 1 (2020)
Career Earnings $187.00 (3rd)
Career Grand Slam N/A — missing: The Open Championship

Robert's lone championship came in 2020, but it was one for the record books — his share of that season's pot is still the highest single-season earnings percentage anyone in league history has posted. He hasn't been back to the top of the season standings since, but he's stayed competitive: a career quality lineup rate that trails only Greg for best in the league, and 43 event wins, keep him firmly in the middle of the pack rather than the back of it. Five career majors leave him one Open Championship shy of the career Grand Slam, a title he's chased almost as long as Carlos has.

William Sullivan

Click photo for full stats
Championships 0
Career Earnings -$483.00 (4th)
Career Grand Slam 2023

William is the league's biggest statistical outlier. He's won more majors than anyone else in the field and was the second member, after Kevin, to complete the career Grand Slam, finishing his in 2023 — yet he's never once won a season championship. His career winning percentage and made-cut rate both sit toward the bottom of the standings, and he leads the league in career diarrhea withdraws. The gap between his peak-week ceiling — seven major titles and a completed slam — and his week-to-week consistency is the defining story of his time in the league.
